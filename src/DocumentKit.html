Master Service Agreement

Terms & Conditions of Engagement

This agreement governs the relationship between Aniket Shinde (The "Developer") and the Client.

1. Detailed Scope & Limitations

The Developer will build the store using a Pre-Built Shopify Theme. This approach ensures stability, speed, and future-proof updates.

Explicit Exclusions

Unless specifically agreed in a separate "Add-On Quote", the following are NOT included:

  • Custom Code: Writing custom Liquid/JavaScript functionality outside the theme's default features.
  • Content Creation: Writing product descriptions, blog posts, or creating graphic assets/banners.
  • Marketing: SEO backlinks, Social Media Management, or Ad Campaigns.
  • Sales Guarantees: The Developer builds the "infrastructure"; Sales depend on the Client's product and marketing.

2. Client Responsibilities

To ensure the 7-14 day timeline is met, the Client agrees to provide:

  • Assets: High-resolution Logo (PNG), Brand Color Codes, and Font preferences.
  • Product Data: Excel sheet with Product Names, Prices, Descriptions, and Images.
  • Credentials: Access to Domain Registrar (GoDaddy/Namecheap) if DNS setup is required.
  • Payments: Timely payment of the Advance and Final invoices.

Delays in providing these assets will result in a day-for-day extension of the project deadline.

3. Payment Terms

  • 50% Non-Refundable Advance: Required to secure the development slot and commence work.
  • 50% Completion Payment: Due upon project completion, BEFORE the Store Ownership is transferred to the Client.

4. Revisions Policy

The project fee includes Two (2) Rounds of Consolidation Revisions during the design phase.

  • Revisions are limited to: Font changes, Color tweaks, Image swaps, and Section re-ordering.
  • Major Re-designs: Changing the entire theme or layout structure AFTER approval is billable at an hourly rate.

5. Liability & Hosting

The store is hosted on Shopify's secure cloud infrastructure.

  • Security: Shopify manages SSL certificates and PCI-DSS compliance.
  • Uptime: The Developer is not responsible for platform outages or server downtime caused by Shopify.
  • Data: The Client is responsible for maintaining secure passwords.

KNOWLEDGE BASE

Frequently Asked Questions

For Clarity & Transparency

Q: Do I need a paid Shopify Plan?

A: Yes. To remove the "Password Page" and allow customers to checkout (pay money), you must have an active Shopify Subscription (Basic Plan). This is paid directly to Shopify.

Q: Is my store secure?

A: 100%. Shopify provides banking-level security (PCI DSS Level 1 Compliant) and free SSL certificates. Your customer's data is encrypted and safe.

Q: Can the store handle high traffic?

A: Yes. Shopify is a cloud-based platform built to scale. Whether you have 10 visitors or 100,000 during a flash sale, the servers will not crash.

Q: Do you guarantee sales?

A: No. I build the "Engine" (The Store). You are the "Driver". Driving traffic (Fuel) and making sales depends on your marketing strategy, product demand, and ad spend.

Q: Can I manage the store myself?

A: Absolutely. Shopify is designed for non-technical business owners. You will be able to change prices, images, and text without writing a single line of code.
